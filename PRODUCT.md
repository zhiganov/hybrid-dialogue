# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Ben Roberts (the convener) and the ~16 participants of his Hybrid Dialogue inquiry: facilitators, dialogue practitioners, and a few civic-tech builders. Mostly non-technical and design-literate, and several are openly wary of extractive, generic, AI-slop interfaces. At least one participant is dyslexic and has said infinite-scroll and chat interfaces make it hard to find where she fits. They open this on a laptop or phone to read the six proposed conversations, feel which ones pull them, and (in Ben's case) decide which to keep, merge, or cut.

## Product Purpose

A shareable artifact presenting the opening set of conversation invitations for the inquiry, generated from the participant survey. It exists so the group can engage with the conversations as something alive and readable rather than a markdown wall, and so the convener can curate them and export the decisions. Success is people actually reading it end to end, feeling invited, and the convener leaving with a curated set. It belongs to an inquiry whose premise is AI as an amplifier of human-to-human dialogue, not a replacement for it.

## Brand Personality

Humane, considered, alive. Warm and unhurried, like field notes kept by someone who cares about the people in them. The voice is plain and human, never salesy or systemy. It should feel hand-made and rooted, the opposite of a dashboard.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI and SaaS dashboards, including this page's own first draft.
- The overused AI "editorial" cliche: cream (#F4F1EA) background, high-contrast serif, terracotta accent.
- Corporate or marketing slickness, brand-deck polish, persuasion energy.
- Dark mode with a neon or acid accent.
- Infinite-scroll and chat-feed interfaces (a named pain point for a participant).

## Design Principles

- Amplify, don't replace: the design serves human dialogue and human judgment. The AI did the legwork; the people decide.
- Grounded in real voices: the artifact is built from what actual people wrote. Show that, don't abstract it away.
- Legibility is respect: clear structure and findability over density or cleverness. Nobody should struggle to see where they fit.
- A draft, not a verdict: it should feel provisional and editable, inviting curation rather than presenting a finished product.
- Rooted, not templated: every choice comes from this subject (conversation, field, invitation), never from a category default.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. High legibility for dyslexic readers: generous spacing, clear sectioning, no infinite-scroll feel, strong type hierarchy, no long unbroken walls of text. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Sufficient contrast, and never rely on color alone for the conversation-type coding (always pair it with a label). Visible keyboard focus. Fully responsive down to phone.

# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Ben Roberts (the convener) and roughly sixteen facilitators and dialogue practitioners taking part in his Hybrid Conversation Toolkit inquiry. At least one is dyslexic, and the group as a whole is sensitive to interfaces that look machine-generated.

Their context: dropping into a slow, small-group conversation over hours or days, often on a phone, in spare moments. They are not all present at once. The job on any visit is to read what others have left since last time, feel the presence of the group through their words, add one considered contribution, and (for the facilitator) eventually harvest the conversation into something to carry forward.

## Product Purpose

An asynchronous, link-shared room where a small group plus Claude (acting as a quiet facilitator) converse over days. The conversation accretes like a thoughtful correspondence; Claude bridges people who were never in the room at the same time. The facilitator distills it into a harvest that exports to a Kumu map.

Success looks like: people actually read each other and add substance rather than reacting fast; the room feels calm and human; nobody mistakes it for a chat app, a dashboard, or a feed; and the facilitator can weave, harvest, and export without friction.

## Brand Personality

Calm, considered, human. The voice is plain, warm, and unhurried. The interface practices amplify-not-replace: it recedes so that people and their words lead, and Claude is a quiet presence rather than a co-star. Three words: calm, legible, human.

## Anti-references

- **Generic chat app** (Slack, Discord): message bubbles, avatars, presence dots, emoji reactions, "is typing" indicators, unread badges. This is async, not live chat, and must not borrow that grammar.
- **SaaS dashboard**: sidebars, stat cards, hero metrics, dense button toolbars. This is a conversation, not an admin panel.
- **AI-slop aesthetic**: Inter or Roboto, purple-to-blue gradients, glassmorphism, glossy cards, gradient text, rounded-everything. The look that reads as machine-made.
- **Social feed**: like or upvote counts, infinite scroll, trending, any performative engagement metric.

## Design Principles

1. **Reading is the primary act, so protect it.** Other people's words are the main event. Layout, type, and rhythm serve the reader first; the chrome recedes.
2. **Amplify, do not replace.** Claude is visibly distinct from people but never dominant. Its contributions read as quiet weaving, not as the loudest voice in the room.
3. **Legibility is respect.** Reading comfort is a first-class constraint, not an afterthought. This serves the dyslexic reader and everyone else.
4. **Deliberate over instant.** Async rewards considered contribution. Nothing should pressure speed, count activity, or reward performance.
5. **Calm by default.** No pulsing, badging, or attention-grabbing. Presence is felt through writing, not through signals.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Legibility-first, designed with a dyslexic reader in the core group. Generous line-height (around 1.6) and a 60 to 70 character measure for body text. A highly legible typeface. WCAG AA or better contrast on all text. Visible, non-color-only focus states and full keyboard operability. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`. No information conveyed by color alone.
